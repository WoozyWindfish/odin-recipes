Butajiru

Butajiru (Pork soup) is another simple but delicious recipe from Japan. It is both hearty and healthy. It is versatile, easy to make, and is great for when you're feeling a bit under the weather. While there are many ways to make this soup, I will be focusing on a version I would often find in my school lunch teaching English at a Japanese public school. I hope you enjoy!

Ingredients

  • Pork
  • Daikon
  • Carrot
  • Potato
  • Oil
  • Burdock
  • Snap Peas
  • Onion
  • Dashi
  • Miso Paste
  • Water

Steps

  1. Peel and cut the potatoes, carrots, and onions into small, bite-sized pieces.
  2. Add oil to the pan and heat. Add the pork and cook on medium.
  3. Once the pork has cooked down, add the peeled vegetables and cook until softened.
  4. Next, add the burdock and snap peas. Cook on medium until softened.
  5. Add 800ml of water and then add a teaspoon of dashi. Bring it to a boil and then sift the scum out.
  6. Set the heat to low and cover for 8 minutes.
  7. Slowly stir in the miso soup and serve!
